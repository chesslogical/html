Is It Legal to Send $2,000 Cash in the Mail?

Simple, clear answers for USPS, FedEx, and UPS (as of November 2025)

USPS (Regular Mail)

  • Legal? Yes, it's legal.
  • You can send cash for legitimate reasons (gifts, payments, etc.).
  • Not recommended due to theft risk.
  • Regular envelope: Only $15 insurance if lost.
  • Safe way: Use Registered Mail at the post office. Declare the $2,000 value and get up to $50,000 coverage, tracking, and signature.
  • USPS recommends money orders or checks instead.

FedEx

Not allowed. FedEx prohibits all cash, currency, and coins.
  • They will refuse or seize the package.
  • No insurance coverage at all.
  • You risk losing the money and your FedEx account.

UPS

Not allowed. UPS prohibits cash and negotiable currency.
  • Package will be rejected.
  • No insurance or liability coverage.
  • Use another method to avoid problems.

Best and Safest Options for $2,000

  • USPS Money Order (up to $1,000 each, fully insured)
  • Cashier's check or certified check
  • Bank wire transfer
  • Zelle, Venmo, PayPal, or Cash App

These are traceable, insured, and zero risk of losing cash in the mail.
