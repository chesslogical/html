Simple Tutorial: Using Lichess to Load PGN, Add/Remove Arrows, and Integrate with Recording/Audiate

This guide focuses on using Lichess.org's free analysis tools to simplify your chess video creation. Lichess has built-in PGN import, move navigation, and easy arrow drawing—making it way simpler than adding arrows in Camtasia. We'll cover loading a PGN, stepping through moves, adding/removing arrows, and a workflow for pausing your screen recording to create AI audio in Audiate, then resuming to play it back. This keeps things streamlined for tutorials or game reviews.

Why Lichess? It's free, no software install needed, and arrows are native (no post-editing required). Pair it with your TechSmith bundle for recording (Camtasia/SnagIt) and AI voice (Audiate).

Step 1: Prepare Your PGN and Lichess Setup

  1. Export your PGN from Hiarcs Chess Explorer Pro (File > Save as PGN) or copy from chess.com (Game > Share > PGN tab).
  2. Go to lichess.org/analysis (or lichess.org/paste for direct import).
  3. Import the PGN:
    • Click the "FEN/PGN" button (under the board, looks like a clipboard icon).
    • Paste your PGN text into the box.
    • Click "Import" or press Enter—the board loads the game.
  4. Optional: For reusable tutorials, create a Study (Tools > Study > New Study), then import the PGN there for chapters/arrows that save.
  5. Tip: Customize board (gear icon): Flip orientation, change piece style, enable coordinates for clarity in videos.

Step 2: Play Through the PGN Step by Step

  1. Once loaded, the board shows the starting position.
  2. Use navigation controls (below the board):
    • « to start, < to previous move, > to next move, » to end.
    • Keyboard: Left/Right arrows for prev/next.
    • Click moves in the list (right panel) to jump to positions.
  3. Step slowly for recording: Advance one move, explain (via audio), then next.
  4. Tip: Enable engine analysis (cloud icon) for eval bars/stockfish lines if reviewing—adds value to tutorials.

Step 3: Add and Remove Arrows

Lichess arrows are easy and appear live on the board—perfect for recording without extra editing.

  1. To add an arrow:
    • Right-click and hold on the starting square, drag to the ending square, release.
    • Default: Green arrow (for plans/attacks).
    • For colors: Hold modifiers while dragging—Alt for green, Shift+Alt for yellow, Ctrl+Alt for red, Alt+Ctrl+Shift for blue.
    • To highlight squares: Right-click on a square (no drag) for green circle; use modifiers for colors.
  2. To remove:
    • Draw the exact same arrow again (it toggles off).
    • Or right-click on an empty square (clears all arrows/circles).
    • For specific removal: No direct way, but redraw to toggle or clear all and re-add needed ones.
  3. In Studies: Arrows save per position/chapter—great for prepared tutorials.
  4. Tip: Practice in a test game. Arrows don't persist after refresh unless in a Study. Use for emphasis like "Black attacks here" with a red arrow.

Step 4: Recording Workflow with Pausing for Audiate Audio

Use Camtasia (or SnagIt for quick tests) to record your Lichess screen. Pause to create/play AI audio seamlessly.

  1. Open Camtasia > New Recording > Select Lichess browser window > Enable System Audio (captures played MP3s).
  2. Start recording > Step to a key position in Lichess > Add arrows as needed.
  3. Pause recording (Camtasia pause button or hotkey).
  4. Create audio in Audiate:
    • Open Audiate > Type script (e.g., "In this position, Black's arrow shows the f5 break...").
    • Generate AI voice > Edit transcript > Export as MP3.
  5. Unpause recording > Play the MP3 (in VLC or media player)—system audio records it while you continue stepping moves/removing arrows.
  6. Repeat for each segment: Pause for new audio, unpause and play while advancing.
  7. Stop recording > Edit in Camtasia: Trim pauses, add effects if needed (e.g., zoom on board).
  8. Tip: For sync, play MP3 slightly after unpausing. If pauses are messy, record silent video first, then overlay Audiate audio in editor. No mic needed—AI handles narration!

Step 5: Export and Upload

  1. In Camtasia: Share > YouTube preset (1080p MP4).
  2. Test playback: Arrows visible, audio clear, no copyright issues (Lichess is free-use).
  3. Upload to YouTube with title/description/tags.

Additional Tips

  • Mobile: Lichess app supports arrows (hold one finger on empty square, drag with another).
  • Advanced: Use Lichess Studies for branching variations, comments, and shareable links.
  • Troubleshooting: If PGN won't load, check format (standard notation). For more help, search Lichess forums or watch YouTube tutorials (e.g., "Lichess import PGN").
  • Why Simpler Than Camtasia Arrows: Native arrows look clean, no post-edit alignment needed.

This workflow minimizes your Camtasia struggles—Lichess handles visuals, Audiate does voice. Start with a short test video. If you need tweaks, let me know!
