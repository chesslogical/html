King's Indian Defense (KID): A Great Dynamic System... But Not Quite "Against Anything"

The King's Indian Defense (KID: 1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 g6 3.Nc3 Bg7 4.e4 d6) is one of Black's most aggressive, universal hypermodern systems, famously used by Kasparov, Fischer, and Nakamura for kingside attacks vs White's center. It's excellent against ~70% of games (1.d4, 1.c4, 1.Nf3, flanks/irregulars), reaching familiar structures: fianchetto Bg7, ...d6 support, ...O-O, then ...e5/...c5 breaks + kingside pawn storm (...f5, ...g5).

Not fully universal: Against 1.e4, it becomes the Pirc Defense (1.e4 d6 2.d4 Nf6 3.Nc3 g6)—sharing ideas but riskier (White space edge, engines slightly favor White). Pair KID + Pirc for full coverage (via 1...Nf6 or 1...d6). Covers all 20 White moves with low memorization: Focus on plans (counterattack kingside, ignore center early).

Pros & Cons of KID/Pirc Complex

  • Pros: Dynamic wins (Black scores ~41-49% in masters), imbalances, fun attacks. Universal vs non-e4; crushes flanks (e.g., 1.b3 Nf6 2...g6).
  • Cons: Theory-heavy main lines (e.g., Classical/Sämisch); White space advantage if unprepared. Not "boring solid" like 1...e6.

How to Play KID as a Universal System

Core setup: ...Nf6 (flexible), ...g6/...Bg7/...d6/...O-O. Branch by White:

White's First Move Black's Response Transposition/Name Key Plan
1.d4 1...Nf6 2.c4 g6 3.Nc3 Bg7 4.e4 d6 KID (main) ...O-O, ...e5/...Nc6, ...f5 attack.
1.c4 / 1.Nf3 1...Nf6 (or g6), then ...g6/...Bg7/...d6 KID vs English/Reti Same as above; often ...e5 early.
1.e4 1...d6 2.d4 Nf6 3.Nc3 g6 Pirc Delay ...g6 if needed; ...Bg7, ...O-O, ...a6/...b5 flank or ...e5.
Flanks (1.b3/1.g3/etc.) 1...Nf6 (or d6/g6) KID/Modern Seize center with ...d6/...e5; develop fast.
1.Nc3 / Knights 1...d6 or Nf6 Pirc/KID Same setup; punish tempo loss.
