Yes: Fight the Boredom—Stick to 1 Main Opening Repertoire to Hit 2000, Experiment Informally

At 1500 aiming for 2000, a narrow repertoire (1-2 openings per color) is the smartest path: It builds deep pattern recognition, consistent middlegames, and "home advantage" faster than switching. You're right—jumping around dilutes progress by forcing constant re-learning. Dive deep now (80-90% serious games), play variety in blitz/casual (10-20%) to fight boredom and broaden subtly. Once at 2000+, expand for versatility.

Narrow vs. Broad Repertoire: Pros & Cons for 1500-2000

Aspect Narrow (Recommended Now) Broad (Later, 2000+)
Improvement Speed Fast short-term gains: Master plans, crush unprepped foes. Slower: Shallow knowledge everywhere.
Rating Potential Reaches 2000 easily (e.g., KID/Pirc player: 1500→2000 in 4 months). Better long-term (diverse structures/plans).
Boredom/Predictability Boring if 100%; fix with informal variety. Low prep risk at club. Fun, flexible; but stalls if not deep.
Real Examples "Stuck one opening → 2000 Elo." GMs: Broad + deep mastery.

Why Narrow Wins at Your Level

  • Openings Matter Less Than You Think: Tactics/endgames/positional play drive 80% gains to 2000. Narrow rep frees time for them.
  • No Weakness from "One Opening": At 1500-2000, opponents don't prep deeply—your mastery trumps variety.
  • Boredom Hack: 1-2 serious games/week in main rep; rest blitz/experiments. Rotate 1 "fun" opening monthly.
  • Proof: KID both sides (w/ Pirc) propelled players to 2000 fast via familiar attacks.
